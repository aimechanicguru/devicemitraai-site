## Community Answer

If an AC trips the MCB when switched on, treat it as a safety issue first. Do not repeatedly reset the MCB. Safe information to collect includes whether the trip happens immediately or when the compressor/outdoor unit starts, whether there is burning smell, water near wiring, visible wire damage, or recent servicing.

Possible directions include compressor or outdoor-unit faults, short circuit or insulation problems, overload, or a protection-device/circuit issue. The exact cause needs physical inspection. Household users should not open electrical panels, bypass the MCB, or upgrade MCB rating to stop tripping.
